Self-taught Vs. College degree

By Aiden Oliver

There's a question that comes to mind everytime people talk about coding and how to get started. Most people say goto to college, get a degree and companies will start hiring you. Others say you don't need to goto college to get hired. All you have to do is make a couple projects, contribute to open source and make a portfolio website for employers to see. I think the best chance for being hired is to be passionate about your work because employers will see that. It's an interesting discussion that I think about sometimes.

I also knew someone who started coding in middle school and already had a job waiting for him after he got out of highschool. He made a couple games, put them on the app store and companies were impressed with what he had made that they started offering him jobs. I'm not sure if he went to college or just went for the job but obviously he didn't have to goto college if he didn't want to. I think the apps he made were mostly made in Objective-C or C++. He was actually in one of my classes in highschool. One day he just walks into class with the thickest book I've ever seen, it had had to be at least a couple thousand pages if not more and he slams it on one of the desks and says "I finally memorized this whole book". I remember being completely speechless. I was like how could he learn all that and it turns out he was the one who made those games on the appstore.
